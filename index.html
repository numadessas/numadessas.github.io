About

DESIGN
WITH
CONTEXT.

I'm Numa — a designer, artist, researcher, and educator based in Brazil. My work sits at the intersection of emerging technologies and design practice, with a particular focus on blockchain ecosystems, NFTs, and the social implications of Web3.

On the practice side, I work with visual communication, branding, digital products, and creative direction — mostly for projects operating in the Web3 space. I build identities, campaigns, and digital experiences that communicate clearly even within complex, technically dense environments.

My Master's research in Design investigates blockchain onboarding education and social impact — specifically how design can make these technologies more accessible, legible, and equitable. I don't separate theory from practice: research informs how I design, and hands-on experience shapes the questions I ask.

I also teach. I develop educational content, workshops, and courses about NFTs and blockchain for different levels of familiarity — from total beginners to practitioners looking to deepen their understanding. Mentorship and knowledge-sharing are as important to me as the work I produce.

What I do

AREAS
OF WORK.

01
DESIGN

Visual communication, branding, digital product design, and creative direction. Working primarily with Web3 projects — from identity systems to interface design, campaign visuals to design systems. Practice-led, research-informed.

02
ART

Digital art and creative exploration, often navigating themes of identity, technology, and the body in virtual spaces. Work published on-chain across multiple platforms including Mallow. Art as a critical and experimental space.

03
EDUCATION

Development of educational materials, workshops, lectures, and courses on NFTs and blockchain — designed for different levels of prior knowledge. Mentorship in blockchain, with a focus on access and practical literacy.

04
RESEARCH

Master's research in Design examining blockchain onboarding education and social impact. How do people enter Web3 spaces? What design choices facilitate or obstruct that access? Where does emerging tech generate or deepen inequality?
